Arithmetic Logic Unit

The Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) is the mathematical component of the CPU. Essentially, it handles any operation that has to do with math. This means addition, subtraction, etc. However, it also has the power to do logical operations or bitwise operations such as AND, OR, etc.

By default, the ALU is set to a state of addition. To make use of it's other computational or logical features, external control signals are turned on to signal to the ALU that we want to make use of a different feature.
